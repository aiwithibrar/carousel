LinkedIn carousels consistently outperform regular posts in reach and engagement. If you haven't started using them yet, you're leaving a massive amount of visibility on the table.

In this guide, you'll learn exactly how to create a carousel that gets noticed from choosing the right topic to designing slides that make people swipe.

## Why LinkedIn Carousels Work So Well

LinkedIn's algorithm rewards content that keeps users on the platform. Carousels do exactly that each swipe is a signal that the viewer is engaged, which tells the algorithm to show your post to more people.

Studies show that LinkedIn carousel posts get **3x more reach** than text-only posts and **2x more engagement** than single images.

## Step 1: Choose a High-Value Topic

The best carousel topics follow this formula:

> **"X things I wish I knew about \[topic] before \[outcome]"**

Examples:

* "7 things I wish I knew about LinkedIn before growing to 10,000 followers"
* "5 mistakes I made in my first year of freelancing"
* "The morning routine that doubled my productivity"

**Why this works:** It's specific, relatable, and promises clear value. Readers know exactly what they'll get.

## Step 2: Write Your Slides

Keep each slide focused on **one idea only**. The structure should be:

1. **Slide 1 (Hook):** Bold statement or surprising fact that stops the scroll
2. **Slides 2-8 (Content):** One point per slide, with a short title and 2-3 lines of explanation
3. **Last Slide (CTA):** Tell people what to do next follow, comment, or share

## Step 3: Pick the Right Design

Design matters more than most people think. A beautiful carousel gets shared; a plain one gets ignored.

Key design principles:

* **Use consistent colors** — stick to 2-3 colors throughout
* **Large, readable fonts** — people often view carousels on mobile
* **High contrast** — text must be readable at a glance
* **Visual hierarchy** — slide number or title should dominate each slide

## Step 4: Add Your Branding

Always include:

* Your **handle** (@yourname) on the first and last slide
* A **profile photo** if possible — it builds trust and recognition
* A consistent **color scheme** that matches your personal brand

Over time, people will recognize your carousels before they even read a word.

## Step 5: Optimize the Caption

The caption is just as important as the carousel. Write:

1. A **hook** in the first line (no one reads past it unless it's compelling)
2. A brief explanation of what the carousel covers
3. A **call to action**  "Save this for later" or "Which tip was most useful?"

**Pro tip:** Asking a direct question in the caption dramatically increases comments, which further boosts your reach.

## Step 6: Post at the Right Time

Based on LinkedIn engagement data:

* **Best days:** Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday
* **Best times:** 8–10am or 12–1pm (your audience's timezone)
* **Frequency:** 3-5 times per week for consistent growth
