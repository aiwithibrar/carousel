You spent hours writing that blog post. It got some traffic, some shares — and then it disappeared into the internet void.

Here's the thing: **your blog post is a goldmine for LinkedIn content**. A single 1,500-word article can become 3–5 LinkedIn carousels, each of which can reach thousands of new people.

## Why Repurpose Blog Posts as Carousels?

LinkedIn's algorithm heavily rewards carousel posts (uploaded as documents). They get:

- **3x more reach** than text-only posts
- **Higher save rates** — people bookmark carousels to refer back to
- **More profile visits** — your handle appears on every slide
- **Longer time-on-post** — each swipe counts as engagement

And the best part: you already did the hard work writing the blog post. The carousel is just a reformatted version of content you already have.

---

## Step 1 — Pick the Right Blog Post

Not all blog posts work equally well as carousels. The best ones to repurpose are:

**Great for carousels:**
- ✅ List posts ("7 tips for...", "5 mistakes to avoid...")
- ✅ How-to guides with clear steps
- ✅ "What I learned" or lessons posts
- ✅ Case studies with a clear before/after
- ✅ Industry insights or surprising stats

**Less ideal for carousels:**
- ❌ Long narrative stories (hard to condense)
- ❌ Very technical deep-dives (too much info per slide)
- ❌ Product announcements (low educational value)

For this guide, let's say you have a blog post titled: **"7 Email Marketing Mistakes Killing Your Open Rates"**

---

## Step 2 — Extract the Key Points

Open your blog post and identify the **core takeaways**. For a carousel, you need:

- 1 strong **title/hook** (your cover slide)
- 5–8 **key points** (one per slide)
- 1 **closing slide** with your name and CTA

From our example blog post, the carousel outline would be:

```
Cover: 7 Email Marketing Mistakes Killing Your Open Rates

1. Using a boring subject line
   Most emails are deleted based on the subject line alone.
   
2. Emailing too frequently
   Sending daily emails trains subscribers to ignore you.

3. No mobile optimization
   60% of emails are opened on mobile. Unreadable = deleted.

4. Generic "Dear Subscriber" greetings
   Personalization increases open rates by 26%.

5. No clear CTA
   Every email needs exactly one job. Give it one button.

6. Ignoring your unsubscribe data
   High unsubscribes signal a content-expectation mismatch.

7. Not A/B testing subject lines
   A 5% improvement in open rate compounds massively over time.

Closing: Follow me for more email marketing tips — @yourhandle
```

**Key rule:** Each slide should have a **title** (the mistake/tip) and **2–3 lines of explanation**. Nothing more.

## Step 6 — Download and Post

1. Click **Download All (ZIP)**
2. Unzip the folder — you'll have PNG files, one per slide
3. Combine them into a PDF using [ilovepdf.com](https://ilovepdf.com)
4. Go to LinkedIn → Create post → Click the document icon
5. Upload the PDF
6. Write your caption (use the hook structure from earlier)
7. Post it

**The whole process:** About 5 minutes once you have your blog post.
