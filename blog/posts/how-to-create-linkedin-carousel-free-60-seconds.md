## Posting Your Carousel on LinkedIn

This is where most people get it wrong. A LinkedIn carousel is uploaded as a **PDF**, not as individual images.

Here's how:

1. Go to LinkedIn → Create a post
2. Click the **document icon** (📄) — NOT the image icon
3. Upload your ZIP file... wait, you need to combine the PNGs into a PDF first

**Free way to combine PNGs to PDF:**
- Go to [ilovepdf.com/jpg_to_pdf](https://www.ilovepdf.com/jpg_to_pdf)
- Upload all your PNG slides in order
- Download the PDF
- Upload to LinkedIn as a document post

Done. Your carousel is live.

## Writing a Caption That Gets Engagement

The carousel is just the visual. The caption drives the algorithm.

Use this proven structure:

**Line 1 (Hook):** Bold claim or surprising stat
> "Most LinkedIn creators are leaving 80% of their reach on the table."

**Lines 2-3:** Brief context or teaser
> "I spent 6 months testing what actually works. Here's what the data shows."

**Call to Action:**
> "Save this post — you'll want to refer back to it."

Asking readers to **save** your post is one of the strongest signals you can send to LinkedIn's algorithm.

## What Makes a Great LinkedIn Carousel

The top-performing carousels all share these traits:

**1. Strong cover slide**
Your cover slide is your headline. Make it specific and promise clear value.
- ❌ "LinkedIn Tips"
- ✅ "7 LinkedIn Mistakes Killing Your Reach (And How to Fix Them)"

**2. One idea per slide**
Each slide should communicate exactly one point. If you find yourself writing more than 3 sentences per slide, split it.

**3. Big, readable fonts**
Most people view LinkedIn on mobile. Use a font size that's readable on a small screen without zooming in.

**4. Consistent branding**
Pick one theme and stick to it. Over time, people will recognize your carousels before they even read the title.

**5. A strong last slide**
Your final slide should always include:
- Your name / handle
- A clear CTA ("Follow for more" / "Save for later" / "Comment your thoughts")

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

**Too many slides:** 7–10 slides is the sweet spot. More than 15 slides and engagement drops significantly.

**No cover slide hook:** Your cover is the only slide people see before swiping. If it's boring, they won't swipe.

**Missing your handle:** People screenshot and reshare carousels all the time. If your name isn't on every slide, you lose the credit.

**Uploading as images instead of PDF:** LinkedIn treats document uploads differently from image posts. The carousel format (with the swipe gesture) only works as a PDF.
